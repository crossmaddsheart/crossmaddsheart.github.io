An exploration of 3 different cities and their surface temperatures

The data I found included the surface temperatures from all over the world, but I decided to narrow it down to 3 different areas. This data set came from Kaggle.com, uploaded by Berkeley Earth. Climate change is still being debated when it’s not a matter of if it’s happening, but the severity of it. A lot of rhetoric around climate change tends to focus on if humans are the cause of it. More on this can be found here. I edited the data a bit more, narrowing down the timeframe and areas. Those .csv files can be found here on my github.

For now, this data only includes dates from 1900-2013 as that is some of the most definite (and really workable) data I had on hand. I pulled it from the website data and the raw .csv can be found hereThe data points are all the beginning of each month (ex ’1900-01-01, 1900-02-01′) and not necessarily the record highs and lows of those months. The .csv files are plotted using the vega-lite package, hosting it with idyll for the framework and github. If you hover over the data, you can see with the tool tip the specific dates and temperatures from that data set. While not much past a century of data, there is still a slight rise over time as the data hits closer to the 2000s. In the Brazil graph, you can see the ranges of temperature become taller and more sporadic, something linked to climate change. In the Russia graph, it doesn’t seem to be hitting as low as often.

Make sure to keep an eye on the range (y-axis), as some have been adjusted for viewing and understanding, but won’t compare well to the next (comparisons are at the bottom). Temperatures are in Celsius.
